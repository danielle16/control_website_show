Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

Is taking part in the study voluntary?

Participation is voluntary, but would be greatly appreciated. You may refuse to participate before the study begins, and discontinue at any time, with no penalty or loss of benefits. If you decide to withdraw from the study, the information that can be identified as you and/or your child's will be kept as part of the study and may continue to be analyzed, unless you make a written request to remove, return, or destroy the information. Your decision whether or not to participate in the research will not impact the afterschool activities.

Will you or your child receive anything for participating?

As a thank you, each participating family will receive a monetary incentive for completing surveys throughout the study: At the beginning of the study ($40 per family), 3-months following the start of the study ($50 per family), 6-month follow-up ($60 per family), and 12-month follow-up ($70 per family). At the conclusion of the study, participating children will be allowed to keep their Fitbit Flex 2, which they will begin wearing at the beginning of and throughout the study.

How will your and your child's privacy be protected?

All data and study related information will be handled and secured by the investigators.

  • All the information recorded on surveys will be confidential (meaning it is private). Some of the questions you answer may be about things you don't want other people to know. The researchers will protect your and your child's privacy by replacing your names with a number that can only be linked to you by members of the research team. The list that shows your name and number will be destroyed once data collection for the study is complete.
  • Once researchers collect information, standard confidentiality procedures will be employed. Electronic data will be stored on a private, password-protected computer. Hardcopies (consent forms) will be stored in a locked file cabinet.
  • A description of this clinical trial will be available on http://www.ClinicalTrials.gov, as required by U.S. Law (you can search this website at any time). Additionally, the researchers may publish results of this study, but your name or any identifying information will not be used. All published results will be presented in summary form only.

What are the possible risks or discomforts from being involved in this study?

The risks and discomforts related to the study are minimal and no greater than what the children would face everyday in terms of physical activity. To ensure that your child does not overexert him/herself by setting unattainable physical activity goals, each goal can only go up to 90 minutes at a time. Any child feeling discomfort during participation may contact their YMCA counselors. You will not be notified immediately by YMCA counselors in this case.
